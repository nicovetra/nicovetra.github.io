Dichotomies

Poem · February 2, 2026
Rolling down the street With some close friends For a cozy northern retreat Out by the lake and the hills Where the day drifts by at our will We'll eat and drink well beyond our fill. But as we pull up to a light, The dichotomies of life hit us in the neck Suffocating the words in my throat. As we pass that unfortunate sight The thick air dries out my eyes Pulling out tears, as would that cold windy night. The sight sends icicles sliding down my back, Yet I am not the one outside in the cold; I'll have warm hot chocolate, in someone's second home As the light flickers to green, it cuts through that thick screen Our words start tumbling and turning anew Like a babbling brook, bursting out of winter blues. We wind along to our final destination, full of excitement Stepping out of the car to stretch Limbs that we had forgotten. As we walk in, the crisp air inside Raises the hair on our hides, But how can I complain, when with a quick flick, we get heat. As we make a rich and lovely dinner I can't help but think back to the man in the street. I even took a quick note, afraid I would forget. Afraid I would be ignorant of the frigid world we live in Cruel and unfair, wanting to see change But terrified to give up our share. I recall the man Three cars ahead Walking to windows Agitatedly shaking his hands, begging. Like a pastor cupping his palms Praying, perhaps asking the lord To help his parishioners. But a pastor lacks such intensity and despair, Seething out of his body Like cornered prey. It struck me more as a patient's' family Crying out to god, delusions of life flowing Through their poor sod. Or a desperate human Begging ancient nobles, down at their feet For just a bushel of wheat, give us this day our daily bread. I could not see the man's eyes, I wish I had. But I could feel the despair Strangle out any conversation. Sinking in our seats, shying away in shame It feels so hard to acknowledge our fellow human And keep them a little more sane. We fear to give, for what it may be used Perhaps it is a risk we must take Since food, and more food Cannot clothe the poor man, out on the street Trembling and begging In the cold, freezing.
